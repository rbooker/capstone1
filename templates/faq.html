Frequently Asked Questions


Q. Are you affiliated with Jeopardy! or JService?

A. No and no. I am a fan of both, however.



Q.Who exactly is this site for?

A. If you read the about page, you'll learn that I made it to aid me in writing quizzes for parties and other gatherings. But it's also for anyone else who wants to do the same. My hope, too, is that it'll help sustain independent pub trivia hosts who are suffering from question-writing fatigue. I'd hate to see yet another bar's trivia night get outsourced to the Geeks Who Drink empire.



Q. Why can't I add my own category to/edit the category of a question?

A. The reason category information is included with the questions obtained from JService - all of which are taken from episodes of Jeopardy! - is that a lot of them rely upon it for context (this information is even referred to as the "JCategory" - a shortening of "Jeopardy! Category"). This, alongside the fact that the quizzes you create for the site are organized by round, not category, means it doesn't really make sense to allow users to add a category to a question, or edit a question's JCategory.



Q. Why aren't there links to edit questions on the Quiz Edit page?

A. Questions exist independently of quizzes, so if you edit a question, the changes occur to every instance of it. In slightly less technical terms: if you re-write a question that's on multiple quizzes it gets altered on every single one of them. Having the links to edit questions on the Questions page rather than on a particular quiz's Quiz Edit page emphasizes this point.



Q. Why do my question IDs not start at one/aren't consecutive?

A. The database stores not just your questions, but the questions of everyone using the site. The ID numbers for questions are assigned sequentially in the order they're added to the database, and the first question you store might be the 4,782nd stored by all users. Similarly, if you add some questions, and then, before you add any more, other users add 100 questions of their own, the enumeration of the IDs of your questions will contain a one-hundred unit gap.
